About Hungary

Hungary is a country in Central Europe known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and thermal baths. The capital city, Budapest, is often called the "Paris of the East" due to its beautiful landmarks and vibrant culture.

Top Attractions

Hungarian Parliament

The iconic Gothic Revival style building on the banks of the Danube.

Buda Castle

Buda Castle is the historical castle and palace complex of the Hungarian kings in Budapest.

Lake Balaton

The largest lake in Central Europe, often called the "Hungarian Sea".

Shoes on the Danube Bank

Waterside shoe sculptures memorializing those killed at the river by a fascist party during WWII.

Heroes' Square

One of the major squares in Budapest, noted for its iconic statue complex.

Fisherman's Bastion

A terrace in neo-Gothic and neo-Romanesque style on the Buda bank of the Danube.

St. Stephen's Basilica

A Roman Catholic basilica named in honour of Stephen, the first King of Hungary.

Great Market Hall

The largest and oldest indoor market in Budapest.

Dohány Street Synagogue

The largest synagogue in Europe and the second largest in the world.

Hungarian Cuisine

Hungarian cuisine is known for its hearty dishes, use of paprika, and delicious pastries. Some popular dishes include goulash, chicken paprikash, and chimney cake (kürtőskalács).
